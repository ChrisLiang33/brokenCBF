How the env actually runs

Phase When What gets created / changed
Sim start once 64 parallel envs spawned. Each gets KMAX=20 obstacle prim slots, a robot, sensors. Geometry is fixed for the run; nothing is destroyed/recreated later.
Episode reset
(per env, when an episode ends)		 fall / collision / timeout Re-randomized on this env (other 63 unaffected):
• Kactual ∈ [0, 20] obstacles teleported into spawn area; the rest parked off-stage
• friction, force/torque, COM offset re-sampled
• robot re-positioned at reset pose
• planner_id re-sampled from weighted distribution
• planner-specific state (goal, waypoint, walk heading) re-sampled
Mid-episode
(every 10 s during episode)		 resampling_time_range fires planner_id re-sampled. Same distribution. The chosen planner re-initializes its internal state (new goal point, etc.)
Per-step
(50 Hz, 0.02 s)		 every step Active planner outputs udes based on its logic. Teacher reads priv obs + outputs CBF parameters θ. CBF QP solves for usafe. Locomotion tracks usafe.

The 10-second resampling exists to increase the diversity of (planner, scene) pairs the teacher sees during PPO training without waiting for an episode to terminate. With 4096 envs × ~150 episodes/env over 3000 PPO iterations, that's ~600k episodes; resampling mid-episode adds another ~600k planner-id transitions to the training distribution. It's purely a data-collection mechanism, not anything the policy needs to handle differently.
