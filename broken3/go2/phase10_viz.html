What you're looking at. CBF math: grad_h · u ≥ φ − α·h. Higher φ pushes the robot away from h=0 (extra margin); higher α relaxes the constraint as h grows (less intervention far from obstacles).

What to look for.
• If trivial collides on a scene but conservative reaches → safety is gated by (φ, α) — adaptation has room to add value.
• If conservative reaches but adaptive is faster → adaptation contributes value by being aggressive in open space.
• If adaptive matches conservative on time-to-goal but conservative beats trivial → adaptation isn't pulling its weight on this scene; the scene only needs "be conservative".
